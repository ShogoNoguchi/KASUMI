# Source card: 2022 young-team proposal on Japan's national public-service workplace

Primary source: Future of Public Service Young Team, Cabinet Secretariat / National Personnel Authority, "Toward a Colorful Public Service: A Sustainable Workplace Where Everyone Can Contribute" (official Japanese PDF, 2022-04-28), https://www.cas.go.jp/jp/gaiyou/jimu/jinjikyoku/pdf/wakate_honbun.pdf

Use this card only as problem context and mechanism inspiration. It is not an adopted policy package, not a causal evaluation, not an empirical evaluation, not a calibration source, and not evidence that any policy field has a real-world causal effect.

## Why it matters for this simulation

The proposal frames the core institutional problem as a shift from a homogeneous, high-context bureaucracy toward a more diverse and resilient public service. It emphasizes that employees want to contribute to society, but routine assignment, all-consuming work, family constraints, and opaque career paths can prevent sustainable contribution.

The proposal groups its recommendations into two broad themes: (1) colorful careers that let diverse employees use and develop strengths, and (2) a workplace that should no longer be called black. The Japan Policy Scientist model therefore represents both career mechanisms and workload/service mechanisms rather than only morale.

## Mechanisms that map to the policy space

- Career choice and career visibility: the proposal motivates more transparent career options and role matching. In the model, this motivates `preference_matching`, `specialist_track_access`, and `learning_access_probability`.

- Voluntary and capability-aware transfers: the proposal argues for moving from seniority-based rotation toward open application and role requirements. In the model, this motivates `preference_matching`, `transfer_capacity_rate`, `transfer_explanation_quality`, and `appeal_channel`.
- Strategic HR and talent management: the proposal calls for HR strategy, strengthened HR capability, and talent-management systems that connect organizational needs with individual circumstances. In the model, this motivates field-aware hiring, onboarding, persistent transfer preference, and management explanation rather than arbitrary reassignment.
- Learning and cross-ministry development: the proposal supports shared learning content, learning communities, and a ministry-wide 20 percent-style project rule. In the model, this motivates `learning_access_probability`, `protected_learning_share`, and skill-stock mechanisms that trade off current delivery against future capacity.
- Management quality: the proposal treats management ability as central to performance and motivation. In the model, this motivates deterministic allocation of finite support, triage, reform, explanation, and specialist-track resources.
- Staffing and job sharing: the proposal says some departments have too few people for their workload and rely on overtime even for recurring work. In the model, this motivates `staffing_buffer`, `manager_support`, `hiring_fill_rate`, `hiring_capacity_rate`, and service-harm guardrails.
- Work-process modernization: the proposal points to digital tools, cloud co-editing, online meetings, reducing paper practices, and modernizing legal/administrative drafting workflows. In the model, this motivates `digital_support`, `process_reform_support`, and `productivity_savings_capture`.
- National Diet and after-hours shocks: the proposal notes that Diet-related work is a major source of sudden after-hours work in central ministries. In the model, this motivates stress episodes, deadline-bearing cohorts, after-hours strain, and service-continuity guardrails.
- Health and family constraints: testimonies describe pregnancy, sleep loss, childcare incompatibility, and cultures that reward unconstrained long-hours work. In the model, this motivates family-constraint profiles, health-protecting actions, sealed self-report, and involuntary-transfer fairness constraints.

## Writing boundary

A final paper may cite this source to motivate why these mechanisms are policy-relevant in the Japanese central-government context. It must not say that the source provides coefficients, effect sizes, prevalence estimates for this synthetic population, or validation of the simulation.
