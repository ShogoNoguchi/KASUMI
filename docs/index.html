KASUMI

An autonomous AI scientist for synthetic bureaucratic personnel-policy research.

KASUMI studies staffing, transfer, training, digital-support, and workload interventions in a simulated public-service organization. It combines AI-Scientist-style research automation with Shachi-style LLM-agent ABM, frozen holdout, verified claims, and automated review.

Why this project exists

KASUMI is motivated by a concrete public-administration problem: personnel systems must balance workload, career development, staffing capacity, fairness, and public-service output. The project does not claim to model any actual ministry. It turns that problem class into a synthetic, auditable AI-science benchmark.

Kasumigaseki personnel strain

Public sources discuss young-official retention, long working hours, career uncertainty, digital-process friction, and mismatches between management and non-management perceptions.

From personnel reform to simulation

Government-adjacent proposals discuss opt-in transfers, HR strategy, talent-management systems, learning support, staffing without overtime assumptions, mid-career hiring, and job sharing.

KASUMI is a methodological prototype. It asks how autonomous research agents could generate, test, audit, and write up synthetic personnel-policy experiments with clear claim boundaries.

How KASUMI is implemented

KASUMI connects an automated research workflow to a synthetic public-service organization. The public release includes the evidence bundle and deterministic replay layer so readers can inspect the results without LLM provider calls.

Generate candidate personnel policies

Staffing, transfer, training, digital support, responsive management, and procedural-justice pathways.

Simulate a stressed public-service organization

Agent-based dynamics produce welfare, service-loss, backlog, headcount, strain, and fairness-related metrics.

Select under guardrails

The primary endpoint must improve while service and fairness guardrails remain satisfied.

Freeze and hold out

The selected policy is tested on unreused random seeds without reselection.

Verify claims

Numeric audit and claim verification bind text claims to evidence artifacts.

Write and review

An AI-Scientist-style writing/review surface turns verified evidence into an anonymous generated paper.

Claim boundary

KASUMI reports synthetic simulation evidence only. It is not a policy recommendation, not a real-world causal estimate, not a personnel system, and not a digital twin of any actual ministry or organization.
